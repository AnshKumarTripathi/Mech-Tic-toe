In 1960, British computer scientist Donald Michie built MENACE (Machine Educable Noughts and Crosses Engine), a Tic-Tac-Toe machine made entirely from matchboxes and colored beads.

Each matchbox represented a game state, and beads inside it represented possible moves. During gameplay, the AI picked a random bead to choose its move. After the game:
